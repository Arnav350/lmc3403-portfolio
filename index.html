Self-Assessment Analysis

How I've grown as a
technical communicator

Starting at the beginning of the semester, my goals were to improve my communication and tone with different audiences, learn how to convey the proper tone in professional settings, and to strengthen my overall communication skills in leadership roles.

01
Rhetoric

The area where I saw the largest growth was rhetoric. In the first project I submitted my resume and a cover letter for Citadel. At that point my cover letter included the same information as my resume and didn’t add much at all.

Through that assignment and the feedback I received I learned that I needed to add information that takes my resume experiences and shows why they would be applicable to the specifics of the job description. One example of this is in my Hulu paragraph where I say, “scale where performance is non-negotiable, much like Citadel’s trading infrastructure.” This is a lot better than having a generalized cover letter that looks good for all SWE based jobs. I also took into account the tone that I needed to apply and went for a more professional and persuasive tone instead of just purely informational like in this sentence, “same kind of prioritization and trade-off reasoning that Citadel engineers apply.“

Another example of this is the third project. In this project we focused on users that were already accustomed to the organization and also new users that were not as familiar with the organization. In this case we chose to use a very clear and welcoming form while also providing an abundance of information to make sure that new volunteers had a full understanding without overwhelming them.

02
Process

Moving on to process, over this year I focused on taking in feedback and refining my work. Previously, I would spend a lot of time on my first version of my assignment and all work I do in general, but having the ability to submit a draft and then receive feedback showed me how important it is to review and improve your work.

One of the best examples of this was for MP2 when we received feedback on our infographic. I was in charge of the design of our infographic and I created it in a way I believe to be perfect. During the revisions we presented to another group and they stated that some of the information could be seen as out of order. This is something I never didn’t even realize and wouldn’t have noticed if they did not point it out. From there it took the time to reorder that section to make sure everything was perceived correctly for a user that had no information about the topic.

Another source of revision was for MP3. At this point in time we have had enough projects and revision in class that I started to really think about how what I wrote is viewed from an outside perspective. For this project we decided to include a certain amount of information on the website in order to not overwhelm the user, but still give them enough information. I then thought about the case where the user has essentially no information about the organization and just stumbled upon the website. In this case they would be very confused since the website starts with the onboarding process and doesn’t explain much if you have no clue what the organization is. These experiences proved that having iterations are helpful even when you don’t think you need them.

03
Modes & Media

The next area I have improved upon is modes and media by working on my communication in multiple formats. This has been a goal of mine since I have the ability to talk formally in written formats, but struggle to implement that in verbal settings and choosing what information to include when creating a visual.

During MP2 we had to condense the most important information when converting from a report to an infographic. At first we wanted to include more information and more details about the events itself, but we took a different approach instead. With our new method we decided to include a low amount of information when compared to other groups and made the text larger. Since our infographic was targeted to a more casual audience we wanted it to be visible and easily accessible. We also decided to include a larger call to action by talking about how these readers could identify deepfakes instead of spreading details about the event since that is what our casual audience truly cares about.

04
Design

Finally, I developed a strong design awareness by focusing on how organization affects my technical communication. As stated earlier, in our infographic we chose to order our sections in a specific way so that each section adds on top of each other and when suggested to rearrange some of the graphics we followed that advice. In this new arrangement the timeline was placed as the first graphic which is more chronologically supportive for the reader even though it doesn’t exactly line up with the text.

In GT Parents with Pups website we wanted to make the onboarding process as easy as possible so that there would be a low barrier to entry for new volunteers. We also wanted to include enough and the most relevant information for users that have not heard about the organization before. These projects showed that a clear hierarchy and logical organization are especially important for audiences to process information efficiently.

Overall, this semester has helped me enhance my technical communication skills. I improved my ability to tailed content for specific audiences, revise and iterate designs, communicate across multiple mediums of media, and use design in specific ways to convey the messages I want. These skills are directly applicable to my future goals in startup leadership where my success is heavily dependent on my proficiency in communicating ideas to users, teammates, and stakeholders.

Class Projects

Major Project 1

Career Materials

In this project I created a resume and cover letter targeting a SWE internship at Citadel. They cover my relevant qualifications and experiences as well as how they are applicable to the job posting. After receiving feedback that my cover letter was too similar to my resume, I revised it to explain why my experiences were relevant to Citadel specifically, not just what I did. This project required me to make a deliberate distinction between two of my job materials that could easily blur together. This showcases my effective technical communication by understanding the purpose of different formats and knowing my audience well enough to give them exactly what they need.

Resume Cover Letter

Major Project 2

Technical Crisis Report & Infographic

For this project I worked in a group to analyze the communication strategies used during the Zelenskyy deepfake crisis, which is where a fake AI generated video shows President Zelenskyy ordering Ukrainian troops to surrender. The report used a rhetorical analysis of primary and secondary sources to evaluate what specific factors made the response effective and what could've been done better. The infographic then took the key findings and calls to action and explained them in a visual format for a more general audience. This project demonstrates my ability to communicate the same topic in different mediums and to different audiences.

Report Infographic

Major Project 3

Community Training Materials

This project addresses a major communication issue within the GT Parents with Pups organization, a volunteer group that has dog therapy events. The report goes over current issues and provides recommendations for the organization. We then created a website to showcase what these recommendations would look like in practice. One critique was that our website lacked information for users that weren't aware of the organization. While we took this into account and provided more context, we decided to prioritize a simple onboarding for new volunteers since they are the main audience. This project highlights my ability to balance the needs of audiences and make strategic decisions based on revisions I have received.
