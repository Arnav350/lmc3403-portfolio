🐾 About the Organization

Lending a helping paw since 2019

When students arrive on campus, they often leave their pets behind. For those who miss their furry friends, GT Parents with Pups is ready to help. Founded by Marla Laminack in October 2019 after she spotted a Reddit post seeking community members willing to bring dogs to campus, the group began with Marla sitting on Tech Green with her chihuahua Nano. The response was immediate and overwhelming.

Today, the organization has grown to over 800 members in its Facebook group and thousands of Instagram followers. At events, dozens of dogs and hundreds of students gather - and Marla even bakes snacks for students who skip lunch to spend time with the pups. The group partners with campus organizations like SMILE and the Ramblin' Reck Club, and co-hosts the Pennies for Sideways event each finals week.

"I had no idea how much these students miss their pets, but we have students coming to us all the time saying how our dogs help fill that hole in their hearts while they're away from home. The dogs love the kids and vice versa. We just try to spread some joy to kids who need it." - Marla Laminack, Founder

Volunteers (both student and dog-owning) are what make every event possible. Your time and your pups make a real difference on campus.

Student Volunteer Guide

Everything you need to know about your role, campus conduct, and event-day responsibilities at GT Parents with Pups therapy events.

1
Understand Your Role
▾

Thank you for supporting the organization! Your role will be to facilitate a welcoming and safe environment for students visiting and for the dogs. You will be managing the check-in table where dog owners will show you proof of current vaccinations. You are also expected to direct foot traffic and greet visitors who join the event.

2
Sign the Georgia Tech Liability Waiver
▾

All student volunteers are required to complete a liability waiver before participating in any event. This is a Georgia Tech administrative requirement, not specific to our organization.

The waiver covers your participation in an event involving animals on campus. You only need to complete this once per academic semester.

⚠️
Required before your first event You will not be able to volunteer on event day without a completed waiver on file. Contact your event organizer at least 48 hours in advance to confirm your waiver status.
  • Download and complete the GT Waiver and Release Form
  • Submit your completed form to your event organizer before the event
  • Keep a copy for your records
3
Review On-Campus Conduct Guidelines
▾

GT Parents with Pups events are held in public campus spaces. As a volunteer, you represent both the organization and Georgia Tech, so professional and considerate conduct is expected at all times.

✅ Do

  • Greet visitors warmly and offer orientation
  • Monitor crowd density near dogs
  • Defer to the therapy dog handler's instructions
  • Speak quietly around the animals

❌ Don't

  • Pet or approach a dog without the handler's permission
  • Allow visitors to overwhelm or crowd a single dog
  • Make loud or sudden movements near the dogs
  • Leave the check-in table unattended during peak hours
4
Safety Protocols & Emergency Contacts
▾

In the rare event of an incident (a dog behaving unexpectedly, a visitor with an allergic reaction, etc.), follow these steps:

  • Calmly ask nearby visitors to step back and give space
  • Immediately notify the therapy dog handler involved
  • Alert the event organizer - do not try to handle it alone
  • If medical attention is needed, call Georgia Tech Police at (404) 894-2500
  • Complete an incident report form if provided by the organizer
📞
GT Police Non-Emergency (404) 894-2500 · Available 24/7 for on-campus incidents

Dog Owner Volunteer Guide

Logistics, safety protocols, and behavioral requirements for therapy dog handlers bringing their dogs to GT Parents with Pups events.

1
Verify Your Dog's Eligibility
▾

Not all dogs are suited for therapy events. Before participating, your dog should meet the following baseline criteria. A therapy dog handler is any parent or community volunteer who brings a certified or approved dog to campus events.

🩺
Vaccination Requirements Your dog must be current on all core vaccinations: Rabies, DHPP (distemper, hepatitis, parvovirus, parainfluenza), and Bordetella. Documentation must be available upon request.
  • Dog is comfortable with strangers and large crowds
  • Dog does not exhibit food aggression or territorial behavior
  • Rabies vaccination current and documented
  • DHPP vaccination current and documented
  • Bordetella vaccination current and documented
2
Parking & Campus Arrival Logistics
▾

Georgia Tech has several designated visitor parking lots available across campus. Event planners arrange parking vouchers or validation codes through GT Parking & Transportation Services (PTS). The following visitor lots are available for use:

  • Visitor Area 1 - E46 / Burge / McAfee / North Avenue Visitor Lot
  • Visitor Area 2 - Student Center Visitor Lot
  • Visitor Area 3 - Lot across from CRC / Health Center
  • Visitor Area 5 - W23 Deck Visitor Lot
  • Visitor Area 8 - GT Hotel & Conference Center / Global Learning Center Lot
📍 GT Campus Parking Map - Visitor lots (shown in blue) are available at Areas 1, 2, 3, 5, and 8. View full map at pts.gatech.edu
3
Dog Safety & Behavioral Protocols
▾

Your dog's wellbeing and the safety of visiting students are both your responsibility during the event. You, as the handler, have full authority over interactions with your dog and can end any interaction at any time.

🐾 During the Event

  • Keep your dog on a 4–6 ft leash at all times
  • Monitor your dog's stress signals continuously
  • Set clear boundaries - you may decline any interaction
  • Bring water and a portable bowl for your dog
  • Bring a mat or blanket for your dog to rest on

⚠️ Stress Signals to Watch

  • Excessive yawning or lip-licking
  • Tucked tail or flattened ears
  • Trying to move away or hide
  • Growling or stiffened posture
  • Whale eye (showing whites of eyes)
🛑
If your dog shows distress signals You are empowered to remove your dog from the space immediately. Notify the event organizer - there is no obligation to stay. Your dog's comfort comes first.
4
Check-In Process & Waivers
▾

Upon arrival, head to the check-in table (a staffed table near the event entrance managed by student volunteers). You'll need to complete the following before entering the event space with your dog:

  • Show proof of current vaccinations (digital or paper copy accepted)
  • Get your assigned area and any updated logistics from the organizer
💡
New to the organization? The event organizer or a senior handler will give you a brief orientation when you check in. You do not need to navigate this alone on your first event.
5
After the Event
▾

When the event concludes, check out with the event organizer before leaving so they can confirm attendance and collect any feedback. You'll also be added to the email list for future events if you aren't already.
